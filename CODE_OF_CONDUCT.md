# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors, maintainers, and community members of CyberTuz pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free, respectful, and constructive experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Behavior That Contributes to a Positive Environment

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community and learners
- Showing empathy and kindness toward other community members
- Helping newcomers learn and grow
- Acknowledging mistakes and learning from them

### Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, trolling, insulting, derogatory comments, or personal attacks
- Public or private harassment of any kind
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission (doxxing)
- Sharing, encouraging, or facilitating illegal use of security tools
- Posting content that promotes unauthorized access to systems
- Sexual or violent content in any project space
- Any behavior that a reasonable person would find inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Ethical Use Enforcement

CyberTuz is a cyber security education tool. The following conduct is **strictly prohibited** within this community:

- Asking for or sharing help with unauthorized intrusions, illegal hacking, or attacks on real systems
- Requesting or providing methods to bypass security measures on systems without permission
- Sharing exploits targeting production systems with malicious intent
- Using community spaces to coordinate or plan illegal activity

Violations of ethical use standards may be reported and will result in immediate removal from community spaces.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing the standards of acceptable behavior. They have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — including the GitHub repository, issue tracker, pull requests, and any other official community channels — and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

---

## Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a private issue or contacting the maintainer directly via GitHub.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The project team is obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Project maintainers will follow these steps when responding to violations:

**1. Correction**
A private written warning with clarification of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate.

**2. Warning**
A formal warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the involved parties for a specified period.

**3. Temporary Ban**
A temporary ban from any interaction or public communication within the community for a specified period.

**4. Permanent Ban**
A permanent ban from any public interaction within the community.
